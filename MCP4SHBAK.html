MCP4SH™

Haptics that feel more like a chassis, and less like chaos.

MCP4SH™ is a SimHub plugin built to make sim-racing haptics more coherent, more readable, and easier to trust. It takes the messier side of telemetry and turns it into a more connected feel across grip, scrub, suspension, drivetrain, braking, and weight transfer style cues.

Built for: sim racers who want clearer information, not just more shaking.
Status: v1.0 release — stable, supported, still evolving.
Canonical home: GitHub for releases, hashes, docs, and ongoing updates.

What it does

  • Layers effects more intentionally so they feel connected instead of random.
  • Improves readability of scrub, suspension, drivetrain, braking, and load-transfer cues.
  • Normalises behaviour across sims so the plugin is less title-specific than typical haptics setups.
  • Reduces re-tuning fatigue by giving you a more portable starting point.

What it is not

  • It is not magic, and it will not make every rig feel identical.
  • It is not “more effects for the sake of it.”
  • It is not finished forever; v1.0 is the release baseline, not the last word.
  • It is not a replacement for sensible gain structure, good mounting, or decent telemetry from the game itself.

Licensing

What the paid version adds

Honestly, the paid value-add is not “more shaking.” It is the adaptive layer that makes the plugin behave more intelligently.

  • ST Tensioner — priority-aware cue management so important events push through more cleanly.
  • ST Balancer — output rebalancing to help the plugin travel better across different games.
  • ST Learner — adaptive shaping of scrub and engine/tyre behaviour.

Why that matters

The goal is better clarity, not more volume. The licensed tools help the plugin manage attention more cleanly when several things are happening at once. That is where a lot of the “connected chassis” feel comes from.

Reality check

Telemetry quality still varies by sim. Some titles are cleaner than others.
Rig layout, mounting, seat construction, and gain structure still matter.
GT7 support is not yet where I want it to be.
v1.0 is stable to ship, but refinement will continue.
