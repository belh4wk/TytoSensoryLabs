TYTO SENSORY LABS

Tyto Sensory Labs was built around a simple idea: technology should not just output data, it should communicate meaning. My background spans military communications, IT, enterprise delivery, live operations, quality, and systems improvement. Across all of it, I kept running into the same problem: the right signal was often buried in noise, scattered across tools, or presented in ways that were harder to use than they should have been. Tyto grew out of that gap. It is a space for building and exploring better sensory systems — ways for machines and software to communicate more clearly through haptics, sound, light, and other human-readable cues. The work started in gaming, simulation, and telemetry-heavy environments, but the idea reaches far beyond them: wherever people need fast, intuitive understanding from complex systems, better communication matters. Tyto Sensory Labs exists to make that communication more natural, more useful, and more human.

MCP4H - The Multimodal Communications Protocol for Humanity

The Human-Centric Governance Layer for AI. > Tyto Sensory Labs introduces MCP4H, a standardized architectural governance toolkit for the Model Context Protocol. By providing a unified schema for multi-sensory data, MCP4H ensures that autonomous agents remain transparent, auditable, and prioritize human intent across any hardware environment or output modality.

MCP4SH - SimHub Plugin

The flagship implementation for SimHub. MCP4SH translates complex racing physics into a layered haptic codec, preventing "signal smear" and providing drivers with intuitive traction and load cues, making for a slightly more refined, usable set of haptics. For any game. Out of the box. Less buzzing, more car.
